Description

Late blight is a serious disease of potato and tomato plants caused by the water mold Phytophthora infestans. It appears as irregular green-black water-soaked spots on leaves that rapidly enlarge into dark brown necrotic lesions.

account_tree

Likely Causes

  • Excessive humidity above 90% for prolonged periods.

  • Cool evening temperatures (10-15°C) followed by warm days.

  • Poor air circulation within the plant canopy.

medical_services

Recommended Treatment

Immediate intervention is required to save the remaining crop.

Step 1

Remove and incinerate infected foliage immediately.

Step 2

Apply copper-based fungicides to healthy neighboring plants.
